Lu Shoukun

Mactaggart Art Collection

Brief BioChinese, 1919-1975 NationalityChinese DescriptionLu Shoukun was a renowned Chinese painter and educator who made significant contributions to the New Ink Movement, a contemporary art movement that reinterpreted traditional Chinese ink painting techniques with a modern approach. Having studied painting in the 1930s under the guidance of the distinguished Chinese painter and calligrapher, Zhao Shao'ang, Lui relocated to Hong Kong in 1948. Thereafter, he focused on studying traditional Chinese painting and later integrated the concept of Western modernism with ink painting. Lui commenced writing articles on art and exhibition reviews for local newspapers in 1952. In 1958, he founded the "New Art Studio" to teach painting. Four years later, he was appointed as an honorary advisor to the Hong Kong Museum of Art. In 1968, Lui and his students established the "In Tao Art Association" and held their inaugural group exhibition at the Chinese University of Hong Kong. In recognition of his contributions to the arts, Lui was awarded an honorary M.B.E. (Member of the Most Excellent Order of the British Empire) by the British government in Hong Kong in 1971. Lui Shou-Kwan's remarkable achievements continue to inspire many in the realm of contemporary Chinese art.

Chang, Arnold

Mactaggart Art Collection

Brief BioChinese American, born 1954 NationalityAmerican DescriptionArnold Chang is a Chinese-American artist, art historian, and connoisseur of Chinese painting. Chang was born in New York City. He received his B.A. from the University of Colorado in Chinese language and East Asian studies in 1976 and studied art history at the University of California, Berkeley for his master’s degree. Chang spent many years studying and collecting Chinese paintings and he was a student studying painting and calligraphy with C.C. Wang. His knowledge and expertise have earned him a reputation as a leading expert in the field. He has lectured extensively and has curated numerous exhibitions of Chinese painting. In addition to his work as a scholar and connoisseur, Chang is also a prolific artist in his own right, and his works have been exhibited at major museums and galleries in the United States and abroad. He is particularly known for his landscape paintings featuring majestic mountains and serene rivers, which blend traditional Chinese techniques with a contemporary sensibility.

Wu Guanzhong

Mactaggart Art Collection

Brief BioChinese, born 1919 NationalityChinese DescriptionWu Guanzhong was a prominent contemporary Chinese artist. While Wu Guanzhong is best known for his exceptional fusion of traditional Chinese ink painting techniques with Western artistic styles, he also created simple yet powerful abstract forms in some of his works. These forms often reflect his unique perspective on landscapes and natural scenes, resulting in a captivating blend of realism and abstraction in his paintings. He enrolled at the National Arts Academy of Hangzhou in 1936, where he received instruction in both modern Western art and traditional Chinese painting from Lin Fengmian and Pan Tianshou. Wu's artistic repertoire comprises landscapes, cityscapes, and everyday scenes, where he incorporates elements of traditional Chinese ink painting and Western art movements such as Impressionism, Post-Impressionism, and Fauvism. In 1947, Wu received a government scholarship to study at École Nationale Supérieure des Beaux-Arts in Paris, France. Upon his return to China, he joined the faculty of the Central Academy of Art in 1950, teaching watercolor and drawing in the Department of Architecture at Qinghua University before moving to Beijing Normal University (later Beijing Fine Arts Normal College) to teach art in 1956. During his tenure, he created numerous works inspired by the natural beauty of different Chinese regions. Wu received several awards and accolades, including being named Officier de l'Ordre des Arts et des Lettres by the French Ministry of Culture in 1991 and appointed as an academician of the Académie des Beaux-Arts in 2002.

Zhao Xiuhuan

Mactaggart Art Collection

Brief BioChinese, born 1946 NationalityChinese DescriptionZhao Xiuhuan is a Chinese painter whose works primarily depict flowers, birds, and other natural subjects in a highly realistic and detailed manner. She blends traditional gongbi techniques (fine, precise brushwork that emphasizes highly detailed and realistic depictions) with modern materials and color palettes, emphasizing tonal values and textures. Zhao studied at the high school affiliated with the Central Academy of Fine Arts in Beijing from 1964 to 1969. In 1985, she became a member of the Chinese Artists Association, and in 1989, she moved to the United States. Her works have been exhibited and collected both in China and internationally, including at prestigious institutions such as the National Art Museum of China, the Chinese Culture Center of San Francisco, and the Chinese Modern Art Center in Osaka, Japan.

Yang Shanshen

Mactaggart Art Collection

Brief BioChinese, 1913-2004 NationalityChinese DescriptionYang Shanshen was a prominent Chinese painter and a leading member of the Lingnan School of Painting. After studying art in Japan at Domoto Art Institute from 1935-1938, Yang moved to Macau in 1941 and established the Xieshe Art Society with Gao Jianfu and Feng Kanghou. Xieshe Art Society sought to modernize traditional Chinese painting by incorporating techniques and ideas from Western art while emphasizing local landscapes, people, and customs. He was known for his use of a technique called "splashed ink," in which he would splatter ink onto the paper to create abstract patterns and textures. Also, Yang's works were characterized by soft, delicate brushwork and a muted colour palette. which helped him to establish a distinct style in the Chinese art world. In 1970, he founded the Chunfeng Painting Society to teach painting and received an honorary degree from the Chinese Academic Research Institute in Taiwan. Yang's contributions to the arts were recognized by the Hong Kong SAR (Special Administrative Region) Government, which awarded him the silver medal "Bauhinia Star" and the Guangzhou municipality Government, which declared him an Honorary Citizen of Guangzhou. Yang's artistic legacy continues to inspire contemporary artists in China and beyond.

Shi Lu

Mactaggart Art Collection

Brief BioChinese, 1919-1982 NationalityChinese DescriptionShi Lu was a prominent Chinese painter and wood engraving artist. Shi was known for his highly textured, dynamic paintings that employed strong colors and bold, expressive brushwork. Shi was originally named Feng Yaheng but later changed his name to Shi Lu in honour of two artists who influenced his work: the 17th-century painter Shitao and the 19th-century writer Lu Xun. He received training in guohua painting (a traditional Chinese style of ink-and-wash painting) at Dongfang Art College in Chengdu in 1934 before enrolling at West China Union University in Chengdu in 1938. After joining the Communist Party in 1946, Shi incorporated elements of social realism into his style. He travelled to India and Egypt as a representative of the People's Republic of China in 1955-56. During the Cultural Revolution, he was publicly denounced and imprisoned for three years due to the “Hei Hua shijian” (The Black Painting exhibition), which criticized his bourgeois ideas and "counter-revolutionary" styles. The experience led to severe persecution and humiliation on him. Although he was released in 1970, his imprisonment caused his health to deteriorate until his death in 1982.

Yu Fei'an

Mactaggart Art Collection

Brief BioChinese, 1889-1959 NationalityChinese DescriptionYu Fei’an (original name Yu Kuizhao) was a prominent Chinese painter and calligrapher. Although he began his career as a journalist, he later shifted his focus to painting and calligraphy. Yu specialized in painting flowers and birds in the gongbi style, which emphasizes clearly defining details in a realistic manner. He extensively studied and replicated ancient masterpieces in the Palace Museum, with a particular emphasis on the works from the Song emperor Huizong's collection, which helped shape his unique style in drawing refined and sophisticated bird-and-flower paintings. Yu utilized fine, pointed brushes to create highly detailed depictions of petals, leaves, feathers, and other natural elements, often creating vivid and vibrant images of flowers and birds. His books on the use of colour in Chinese painting in the gongbi technique have been highly regarded and are significant contributions for they provided valuable references to the field of gongbi bird-and-flower painting.

Wang Ziwu

Brief BioChinese, born 1936 NationalityChinese DescriptionWang Ziwu was a Chinese painter renowned for his figure portraits. Wang Ziwu focused on studying the painting techniques of Qi Baishi, Huang Binhong, and Pan Tianshou from 1966 and copied the works of Russian realistic artists like Lebin and Vasily Surikov. Lai Shaoqi

Mactaggart Art Collection

Brief BioChinese, 1915-2000 NationalityChinese DescriptionLai Shaoqi, hailing from Puning, Guangdong province, is renowned for his woodblock prints and paintings. He began studying woodcutting techniques after receiving his degree from the Guangzhou Academy of Fine Arts in 1936. He later became a prominent member of the New Woodcut Movement, which was initiated by the renowned writer and scholar Lu Xun, during the 1930s and 1940s. Lai received guidance and encouragement from Lu Xun, who also introduced his works to the "Complete World Art" (世界美術全集) published in Japan in 1936, as well as to the Soviet Union, France, and England. Lu Xun praised Lai Shaoqi and his peers as "the most combatant young woodcutters.” Lai's woodblock prints typically feature landscapes and natural scenes, with an emphasis on the interplay of light and shadow. Furthermore, he has produced prints that depict various cultural and historical events that reflect the theme of the War of Resistance, which was the prolonged conflict between China and Japan that began with the Marco Polo Bridge Incident in 1937 and culminated in Japan's capitulation in 1945.

Ma Shouzhen

Mactaggart Art Collection

Brief BioChinese, 1548-1604 NationalityChinese DescriptionAs an elite courtesan in the Ming Dynasty (1368 - 1644), Ma Shouzhen (1548 - 1604) was skilled in many fine arts, including poetry, reciting classical literature, dancing, singing, acting, calligraphy and painting. She was born in 1548 in Nanjing, which was known as Jinling at the time, and lived in the popular “pleasure district” along the Qinhuai River. Ma was also known by her style names Yuejiao, meaning “Lunar Beauty”, and Xianglan, which means “Orchid of the Xiang River” and alludes to one of her favourite subjects to paint - orchids. Orchids were popular painting subjects in relation to courtesans as they represented something fragrant, delicate, and beautiful. Ma also frequently painted bamboo, narcissus, and other literati-approved foliage - which are references to the classical past, virtue and eroticism, but also grew readily in the temperate Jiangnan region. Ma Shouzhen began painting around the age of 16 and many of her works were dedicated to her literati clients, including poet and calligrapher Wang Zhideng (1535 - 1612), with whom she had a long-term relationship. The literati, or scholar-officials, wanted to be surrounded by courtesans who were talented and proficient in their areas of interest, including art, prose, and poetry - and Ma’s paintings were in high demand. Courtesans of the Late Ming straddled the dichotomies of masculine and feminine, acting as an intermediary between classes and genders, and were producers and audiences of many different art forms.

University of Alberta E.H. Strickland Entomological Museum

Common NameEdith's Checkerspot SeasonalityAdults fly from June to August, peaking in July. IdentificationThe colour pattern is variable in this species. The upperside is black with orange and pale yellow or white bands. The submarginal band consists of small yellow or white spots and the postmedian band is wide and orange. Ventral hindwing has alternating bands of yellow-orange and cream. It is smaller (wingspan 32-51 mm), darker on the ventral surface, and has more rounded forewing tips than the similar species: Anicia Checkerspot (E. anicia). Subspecies beani, named after Thomas Bean of Lake Louise, occurs in the mountains. The Cypress Hills population is of uncertain taxonomic affinity, but has been referred to as E. editha near subspecies hutchinsi (Bird et al 1995). The eggs are yellow (Guppy & Shepard, 2001). Mature larvae are black and white with orange lateral lines at the base of bristles (Layberry et al., 1998; Guppy & Shepard, 2001). The pupae are not described.

Courtesans of the Late Ming straddled the dichotomies of masculine and feminine, acting as an intermediary between classes and genders, and were producers and audiences of many different art forms.

Euphydryas editha

University of Alberta E.H. Strickland Entomological Museum

Common NameEdith's Checkerspot SeasonalityAdults fly from June to August, peaking in July. IdentificationThe colour pattern is variable in this species. The upperside is black with orange and pale yellow or white bands. The submarginal band consists of small yellow or white spots and the postmedian band is wide and orange. Ventral hindwing has alternating bands of yellow-orange and cream. It is smaller (wingspan 32-51 mm), darker on the ventral surface, and has more rounded forewing tips than the similar species: Anicia Checkerspot (E. anicia). Subspecies beani, named after Thomas Bean of Lake Louise, occurs in the mountains. The Cypress Hills population is of uncertain taxonomic affinity, but has been referred to as E. editha near subspecies hutchinsi (Bird et al 1995). The eggs are yellow (Guppy & Shepard, 2001). Mature larvae are black and white with orange lateral lines at the base of bristles (Layberry et al., 1998; Guppy & Shepard, 2001). The pupae are not described.
